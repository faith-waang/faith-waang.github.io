Nooz



The Issue

News is an important part of everyday life, an element that keeps us all connected to one another across the world. However, in a world of ever-evolving technology and entertainment, traditional news sources like newspaper and broadcast tv has lost its audiences. So, the question was asked—how do we encourage users to engage with the news in an interactive and advanced way?



My Team

I worked in a team of four talented individuals, each with unique skills. We were given two weeks to ideate and develop a basic product and pitch for our solution. Within this team, we had two designers—I was the designer tasked to build the interface of the app, and the other designer created the graphics for our product. The interface was designed on Figma, and graphics developed on Illustrator.



Our Goals

Our team wanted to accomplish three goals in the creation of our product.
1. Display the news in an engaging way for users to interact with.
2. Only show users news that is relevant to them.
3. Allows users to contribute to the news community to instill a sense of ownership.



Our Solution

Nooz is a news platform on which personal daily decks of news articles are curated for each user depending on their preferences. Inspired by interactive apps like Tinder to capture readers' attentions, the Nooz app presents users with daily decks in which they can swipe left (like) or right (to dislike) in order to learn about current events and update their personal preferences.
